Pulmonary Function Testing (PFT)
Pulmonary Function Testing (PFT) is a safe, non-invasive test that measures how well your lungs are working. It evaluates breathing capacity, airflow, and how effectively your lungs move air in and out. This test is commonly used to help diagnose and monitor conditions such as asthma, chronic cough, wheezing, shortness of breath, and other respiratory concerns. The results allow your provider to better understand your symptoms and create the most appropriate treatment plan.


At our facility, testing is performed using the MiniBox+ system by Pulmonone, an advanced pulmonary testing device designed to provide highly accurate results while keeping the experience comfortable and easy for patients. Unlike traditional pulmonary testing equipment that may require sitting inside a closed chamber or performing forceful breathing maneuvers, the MiniBox+ uses modern flow-based technology that allows testing to be completed in a more natural and relaxed manner.
This system is especially suitable for pediatric patients, elderly individuals, and patients who may have difficulty performing standard pulmonary tests, as it requires less strain while still delivering precise and reliable measurements. The MiniBox+ allows for faster testing, improved comfort, and excellent accuracy, helping ensure that each patient receives high-quality diagnostic evaluation.

What to Expect During Pulmonary Function Testing

Pulmonary Function Testing usually begins with spirometry, a simple breathing test performed using specialized equipment that measures how well your lungs move air in and out. During the test, you will breathe through a mouthpiece while a trained respiratory professional guides you through a series of easy breathing maneuvers. The test is safe, painless, and typically takes about 15–30 minutes to complete.
Depending on your symptoms and your physician’s order, additional breathing tests may be performed to obtain more detailed information about your lung function. All tests are non-invasive and designed to be as comfortable as possible for patients of all ages.
After the evaluation, the results help your provider determine the best treatment plan, which may include medications, inhaler guidance, breathing therapy, or lifestyle recommendations to improve your respiratory health. Our team is dedicated to providing accurate testing, clear instructions, and supportive care throughout your visit.

Referral Requirements
Pulmonary Function Tests are typically performed with a referral from your healthcare provider. The referral should include the reason for testing and the specific test requested. If you are unsure whether a referral is needed, please contact our office and our staff will assist you.

Report Turnaround Time

Indications for Pulmonary Function Testing
Pulmonary Function Tests may be recommended for patients with:
Shortness of breath
Chronic cough
Wheezing
Asthma or suspected asthma
COPD / emphysema
Lung disease monitoring
Pre-operative evaluation
Occupational or school clearance
Follow-up after respiratory illness
Abnormal chest X-ray or CT scan
Your provider may request different types of testing depending on your symptoms and medical history.

Contraindications (Who Should NOT Take the Test)
Pulmonary Function Testing may not be recommended for patients with certain conditions, including:
Recent heart attack
Recent eye, chest, or abdominal surgery
Uncontrolled high blood pressure
Active chest pain
Severe shortness of breath at rest
Recent pneumothorax (collapsed lung)
Active respiratory infection (if severe)
Inability to follow breathing instructions
Patient safety is our priority. Our provider will review your history before testing to make sure the procedure is safe for you.

FAQs – Pulmonary Function Testing (PFT)
Are pulmonary function tests painful?
No. Pulmonary function tests are not painful. The test only involves breathing through a mouthpiece while following simple instructions. Some patients may feel slightly tired from blowing out forcefully, but the test is safe and well tolerated.

Can I eat or drink before my PFT appointment?
Yes, you may eat and drink before your test, but we recommend avoiding heavy meals right before your appointment. A full stomach may make it harder to take deep breaths comfortably. It is also best to avoid alcohol and smoking before the test.

Should I use my inhaler before the test?
This depends on your doctor’s instructions. Some patients are asked to stop using certain inhalers before testing so the results are more accurate. Please follow the instructions given by your physician or our office before your appointment.

What should I wear to my PFT appointment?
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows you to breathe easily. Avoid tight clothing around your chest or abdomen, as this may affect your breathing during the test.

Can elderly patients do pulmonary function testing?
Yes. Our laboratory uses modern equipment designed to make testing easier for elderly patients. The test is non-invasive and performed with guidance from trained respiratory professionals to ensure comfort and accuracy.

What if I cannot blow hard enough during the test?
Our respiratory staff will guide you step-by-step and allow you to practice before the actual test. The goal is to get the best effort possible, and the test can be repeated if needed to ensure accurate results.

Can I take my regular medications before the test?
You may take your regular medications unless your doctor tells you otherwise. If you are unsure, please contact our office before your appointment for instructions.

Is pulmonary function testing safe for children?
Yes. Pulmonary function testing is safe for children when ordered by a physician. We use advanced equipment that is designed to be more comfortable and easier for pediatric patients to perform.

Will I get my results the same day?
Results are usually reviewed by the physician who ordered the test. In most cases, your doctor will discuss the results with you at your follow-up visit or contact you after reviewing the report.

Can I do PFT testing without a doctor’s order?
No. All pulmonary function tests require a referral from a licensed physician. A prescription must be provided before scheduling and must be presented at the time of your appointment.
