Meet Dr. Farri

Dr Folashade Farri, MD, MPH
Dr. Folashade Farri is a dedicated Pediatric Pulmonologist and the Specialty
Practice Owner of Children’s Lung, Asthma & Pulmonary Specialists in Wayne,
New Jersey. With a deep passion for helping children breathe easier and stay
active, she combines extensive clinical expertise with a public health background
to provide comprehensive, compassionate care to her patients and their families.
Clinical Leadership & Experience
Throughout her career, Dr. Farri has held several prominent leadership roles in
pediatric respiratory care. She previously served as the Director of the Cystic
Fibrosis Foundation affiliate center at RWJ-Barnabas Health. Prior to that, she
was the Director of the Technology Dependent Children’s Program at Montefiore
Medical Center, where she led a multidisciplinary outpatient program for
medically complex patients. Currently, she extends her impact beyond the clinic
as the Chief Medical Advisor for AIRnyc.

Education & Credentials
Dr. Farri’s rigorous medical training spans international and top-tier US
institutions:
● Medical Degree: Obafemi Awolowo University, Nigeria.
● Master of Public Health: Columbia University, New York.
● Pediatric Residency: Bronx Lebanon Hospital, New York.
● Pediatric Pulmonology Fellowship: Westchester Medical Center, New York.
She is a dual board-certified Fellow of the American Board of Pediatrics and the
American Board of Pediatric Pulmonology. Furthermore, she holds active
professional medical licenses in both New Jersey and New York.

Awards & Professional Recognition
Dr. Farri is highly regarded by her peers and the broader medical community:
● Fellow, American Academy of Pediatrics
● Member, American Thoracic Society
● Member, New Jersey American Academy of Pediatrics
● Member, Asthma, American Academy of Asthma, Allergy & Immunology
● Board Member: Asthma & Allergy Foundation of America
● Awards: Best Faculty Award, RWJ Barnabas Department of Pediatrics
(2021 and 2022).

Beyond the Clinic
Dr. Farri believes deeply in community health and engagement. When she is not
treating patients or scaling asthma quality improvement projects across hospital
systems, she actively volunteers her time locally with the Bethany Community
Center, Washington Township, and the Special Olympics of New Jersey and
internationally on medical missions.
